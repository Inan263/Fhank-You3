A soccer formation is a strategic arrangement of players on the field, designed to optimize the team's strengths and counteract the opponent's tactics. Formations are typically described in terms of the number of players in each line of defense, midfield, and attack, such as the popular 4-4-2 formation, which consists of four defenders, four midfielders, and two forwards. The choice of formation can significantly impact a team's style of play, with some formations emphasizing defensive solidity, while others focus on attacking prowess or midfield control.
